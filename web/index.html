The opening

A price gap that exists only because the asset is tokenized.

xStocks trade around the clock, but the US market is open only ~6.5 hours per weekday. Overnight, on weekends, and on holidays the token has no underlying price discovery — it drifts on crypto-market sentiment, then snaps toward fair value at the next US open. That gap is real risk for anyone holding or making markets in tokenized stocks, and a tradable edge for an agent that respects it.

The loop

Seven stages from market state to a logged decision.

Each tick flows through the same deterministic pipeline. The LLM only gates conviction — it never gets to skip the risk governor.

01

Session clock

Classify the US session; gate the edge to off-hours only.

02

Dislocation

Measure the token-vs-fair-value gap in volatility units.

03

Fair value

Blend 24/7 proxies, damped by their own confidence.

04

LLM gate

Qwen: fadeable noise, or justified repricing — stand down.

05

Risk governor

Size by confidence under a tighter off-hours cap; halt on drawdown.

06

Glass box

Append the decision to a sha256 hash chain.

07

Hedge router

Route a short to the Bitget stock perp the token can’t carry.

Built on Bitget’s real instruments

Signal on the token, execution where shorts are possible.

xStocks — spot only

Tokenized US stocks (e.g. TSLAx) trade as spot swaps on Bitget Onchain / Wallet. They’re long-or-flat — there’s no native short — so GapGuard rests a long discount on the token itself.

venue · onchain-spot

Stock perps — the hedge

To fade a rich gap, GapGuard routes the short to the matching USDT-margined stock perpetual (TSLAUSDT, isRwa), which is shortable and trades ~24/7 on Bitget’s derivatives API.
